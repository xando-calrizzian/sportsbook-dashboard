What this means
Bankroll
The strategy's running paper balance. Starts at $100 (a flat 100-unit bankroll where 1 unit = $1) and updates as bets settle.
Settled vs Pending
Settled = bet has been graded (won, lost, pushed, or voided). Pending = bet placed but the game has not finished yet.
Win rate
Wins divided by decisive bets (excludes pushes and voids).
Efficiency
Profit per dollar risked. If a strategy staked $100 in total and made $58, efficiency is +$0.58 per $1 risked. This is a different number than profit-vs-start.
Premise
The hypothesis behind the strategy. Why it should work in theory. Locked at strategy creation and never edited.
Today: deployed / reserve / skipped
For Kelly-sized strategies. Deployed is the total dollars staked across today's qualifying picks. Reserve is the part of bankroll that stayed unbet (the daily-pool model holds 20 percent of bankroll back, plus any unallocated slack). Skipped is the number of picks that passed the strategy filter but had no edge against real-book consensus odds, so the model declined to bet.
Daily pool (80/20)
Each strategy stakes at most 80 percent of its current bankroll on any one day. The remaining 20 percent rolls forward. Individual bet sizes come from a 1/4 Kelly fraction against real-book consensus probability, capped at 25 percent of bankroll per bet.

Units = $1 each. All figures are paper money, no real bets placed.
