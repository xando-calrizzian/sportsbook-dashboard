What this means
Total value
The strategy's true net position: bankroll in play plus savings locked. Starts at $100 (1 unit = $1). This is the headline number per card.
Bankroll in play
The chips the strategy will deploy on the next day's bets. Gets fully staked each day across selected picks.
Savings locked
Pulled out of the bankroll each night. 20 percent of every profitable day's ending bankroll moves into savings and is never bet again (capped so next-day bankroll never falls below today's start).
Settled vs Pending
Settled = bet has been graded (won, lost, pushed, or voided). Pending = bet placed but the game has not finished yet.
Win rate
Wins divided by decisive bets (excludes pushes and voids).
Efficiency
Profit per dollar risked. If a strategy staked $100 in total and made $58, efficiency is +$0.58 per $1 risked.
Premise
The hypothesis behind the strategy. Why it should work in theory. Locked at strategy creation; sizing model can change but the filter logic does not.
EV per dollar
Expected profit per $1 staked: real-book decimal odds times estimated true probability, minus 1. Picks below the strategy's EV floor are filtered out before sizing.
Today line
What the model did today: dollars deployed, count of bets placed, the top pick's EV/$, and how many picks were skipped for being too chalky (-250 floor) / too low EV / cut at the top-K cap.
Fallback rate
The fraction of today's staked bets that used HC's quoted odds because the Odds API had no real-book match for that pick. A high fallback rate means today's sizing is provisional.
Per-bet cap
No single bet exceeds 15 percent of bankroll (20 percent for the Fortress strategy). Concentration risk control.

Units = $1 each. All figures are paper money, no real bets placed.
